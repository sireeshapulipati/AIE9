# Sarah's 30-Day Vegan Meal Plan for Energy & Mood
*Designed for chronic condition management and morning energy optimization*

## Weekly Themes Overview
- **Week 1**: Whole Foods & Greens Foundation
- **Week 2**: Anti-Inflammatory Powerhouse
- **Week 3**: Omega-3 Rich Energy Foods
- **Week 4**: Comforting Easy-Digest Meals

---

## Week 1: Whole Foods & Greens Foundation

### Sample Daily Menu:
**Breakfast Options:**
- Overnight oats with mixed berries, chia seeds, and almond butter
- Green smoothie with spinach, banana, mango, and plant protein
- Avocado toast on whole grain bread with hemp hearts

**Lunch Options:**
- Quinoa power bowl with mixed greens, roasted chickpeas, cucumber, and tahini dressing
- Lentil and vegetable soup with side of whole grain crackers
- Large salad with mixed beans, nuts, seeds, and nutritional yeast

**Dinner Options:**
- Stir-fried tofu with broccoli, bell peppers, and brown rice
- Black bean and sweet potato tacos with avocado
- Mediterranean chickpea pasta with spinach and cherry tomatoes

**Energy Snacks:**
- Apple slices with almond butter
- Mixed nuts and dried fruit trail mix
- Hummus with carrot and cucumber sticks

### Prep Tips:
- Cook quinoa and lentils in batches
- Pre-wash and chop vegetables
- Make overnight oats 3 days at a time

---

## Week 2: Anti-Inflammatory Powerhouse

### Key Ingredients: Turmeric, ginger, leafy greens, berries, garlic

### Sample Daily Menu:
**Breakfast Options:**
- Golden turmeric smoothie with mango, ginger, and coconut milk
- Chia pudding topped with blueberries and walnuts
- Steel-cut oats with cinnamon, berries, and ground flaxseed

**Lunch Options:**
- Anti-inflammatory lentil soup with turmeric and ginger
- Spinach and chickpea curry with brown rice
- Rainbow salad with beets, carrots, and anti-inflammatory dressing

**Dinner Options:**
- Turmeric roasted vegetables with quinoa
- Ginger garlic stir-fry with tempeh and leafy greens
- Anti-inflammatory vegetable curry with whole grain naan

**Healing Snacks:**
- Golden milk latte (turmeric, ginger, plant milk)
- Dark berries with nuts
- Green tea with lemon

### Prep Tips:
- Make large batch of anti-inflammatory soup
- Prep golden milk paste for easy lattes
- Pre-make curry spice blends

---

## Week 3: Omega-3 Rich Energy Foods

### Key Ingredients: Chia seeds, flaxseeds, walnuts, hemp hearts, algae

### Sample Daily Menu:
**Breakfast Options:**
- Chia pudding parfait with layers of fruit and nuts
- Flaxseed pancakes with walnut butter and berries
- Omega smoothie bowl topped with hemp hearts and seeds

**Lunch Options:**
- Walnut and spinach salad with flax oil dressing
- Chia seed crackers with avocado and sprouts
- Hemp heart energy bowl with quinoa and vegetables

**Dinner Options:**
- Walnut-crusted portobello mushrooms with roasted vegetables
- Omega-rich pesto pasta (made with walnuts and hemp hearts)
- Stuffed bell peppers with chia seed quinoa mixture

**Brain-Boosting Snacks:**
- Walnut and date energy balls
- Flaxseed crackers with nut butter
- Chia seed pudding cups

### Prep Tips:
- Soak chia seeds overnight for easy use
- Make large batch of omega-rich pesto
- Pre-portion nuts and seeds for easy adding to meals

---

## Week 4: Comforting Easy-Digest Meals

### Focus: Cooked foods, warming spices, easy-to-digest preparations

### Sample Daily Menu:
**Breakfast Options:**
- Warm oatmeal with stewed apples and cinnamon
- Millet porridge with vanilla and berries
- Gentle green smoothie (more fruit, less fiber)

**Lunch Options:**
- Creamy butternut squash soup with coconut milk
- Well-cooked vegetable and lentil stew
- Soft quinoa pilaf with roasted root vegetables

**Dinner Options:**
- Gentle vegetable curry with coconut milk
- Slow-cooked bean and vegetable soup
- Steamed vegetables with tahini sauce over rice

**Soothing Snacks:**
- Herbal tea with rice cakes and nut butter
- Stewed fruit compote
- Warm plant milk with turmeric

### Prep Tips:
- Use slow cooker for hands-off cooking
- Steam or lightly cook vegetables
- Focus on warm, comforting temperatures

## Quick Energy Boosters (Any Week):

### 5-Minute Energy Snacks:
- Banana with 2 tbsp nut butter
- Green smoothie (spinach, banana, plant milk)
- Handful of dates stuffed with almond butter
- Energy balls (dates, nuts, coconut)
- Toast with avocado and hemp hearts

### 15-Minute Energizing Meals:
- Quinoa bowl with pre-cooked beans and quick vegetables
- Smoothie bowl with toppings
- Hummus wrap with vegetables
- Chia pudding with fruit and nuts

---

## Meal Timing for Energy:
- **Upon Waking**: Glass of water with lemon
- **Breakfast (7:30 AM)**: Protein + healthy fats + complex carbs
- **Mid-Morning (10 AM)**: Light snack if needed
- **Lunch (12:30 PM)**: Balanced meal with plenty of vegetables
- **Afternoon (3 PM)**: Energy-sustaining snack
- **Dinner (6:30 PM)**: Lighter, easy-to-digest meal
- **Evening**: Herbal tea, no late eating

## Key Nutrients Checklist:
**Daily Essentials:**
- [ ] B12 supplement or fortified foods
- [ ] Iron-rich foods + vitamin C
- [ ] Omega-3 sources (chia, flax, walnuts)
- [ ] Leafy greens
- [ ] Protein source at each meal
- [ ] Colorful vegetables/fruits

## Shopping List Staples:
**Proteins**: Lentils, chickpeas, black beans, tofu, tempeh, quinoa
**Healthy Fats**: Avocados, nuts, seeds, nut butters, tahini
**Energy Carbs**: Oats, brown rice, sweet potatoes, fruits
**Superfoods**: Chia seeds, flaxseeds, hemp hearts, nutritional yeast
**Anti-inflammatory**: Turmeric, ginger, leafy greens, berries

*Remember: This is a flexible guide. Listen to your body and adjust portions and foods based on your energy levels and preferences each day.*
